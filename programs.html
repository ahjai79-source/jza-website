Programs & Enrichment

Beyond the classroom, we offer experiences that build skills, confidence, and character.

Chess
STRATEGY · FOCUS · CONFIDENCE
+

For 6–10 year olds, chess is a quiet superpower. Every match teaches them to slow down, think a few moves ahead, and stay calm under pressure, patience, planning, and the thrill of figuring it out for themselves. Win or lose, they walk away a little more confident in their own mind.

Soccer
TEAMWORK · FITNESS · DISCIPLINE
+

There's nothing like a game on the grass to light kids up. At this age, soccer builds strong, healthy bodies and the social muscles that matter just as much, passing instead of going it alone, cheering a teammate on, bouncing back after a missed shot. They learn that showing up and trying together is its own kind of win.

Financial Literacy
MONEY SMARTS FOR YOUNG ENTREPRENEURS
+

Money doesn't have to be a grown-up mystery. Through hands-on play, saving, spending, and making little choices, kids start to understand where money comes from and how to make it work. It's early confidence with numbers and decisions that quietly sets them up for a lifetime of smart, independent thinking.

Young Markets
LEARN · INVEST · GROW
+

Our Young Markets turn big ideas into real little businesses. Kids dream up a product, make it, price it, and sell it to a cheering crowd, and somewhere in the middle they discover they're capable of so much more than they thought. It's creativity, courage, and that proud spark of “I made this myself.”

Martial Arts
FOCUS · RESPECT · SELF-DEFENSE
+

Martial arts is so much more than kicks and blocks. For this age group it's a beautiful lesson in self-control, focus, respect for others, and the steady confidence that comes from earning each new belt. Kids learn to stand a little taller, listen a little closer, and trust that they can handle hard things.

Art & Creativity
EXPRESS · IMAGINE · CREATE
+

Give a child paint, paper, and a little space to play, and watch their whole world open up. Art is where they put big feelings into something they can see, try ideas with no wrong answers, and learn that mistakes are just the start of something new. It's joyful, messy, confidence-building proof that their imagination matters.
